# Brand Identity & App Naming Strategy

Choosing a name is critical as it sets the tone for your brand identity and UI theme. Since names like **Horizon**, **Orbit**, and **Strata** are indeed very common and heavily trademarked in the software space, we need a name that is unique, legally safe, and embodies the concept of a *"canvas without edges."*

Here are a few naming directions, along with how each would influence the UI theme and brand identity.

## Direction 1: The Infinite Expanse
Focusing on the boundless nature of the canvas, space, and freedom of thought.

*   **Aethera Canvas** (or just **Aethera**)
    *   *Meaning:* Derived from "Aether," the pure upper air breathed by the gods in mythology; represents void and space.
    *   *UI Theme Influence:* Deep, dark purples and midnight blues (like our current Obsidian theme). UI elements feel weightless, utilizing heavy glassmorphism and subtle glowing auras around active tools.
*   **Vast Notes** (or **VastPad**)
    *   *Meaning:* Unbounded, immense, a massive space for creation.
    *   *UI Theme Influence:* Extreme minimalism. Very thin UI borders, maximizing the canvas area. Monochromatic color palette with a single stark accent color (like stark white or neon cyan).
*   **Continuum**
    *   *Meaning:* A continuous sequence in which adjacent elements are not perceptibly different from each other.
    *   *UI Theme Influence:* Fluid animations. UI menus that seamlessly morph into one another rather than popping up. Gradients that stretch across the screen.

## Direction 2: Clarity & Light
Focusing on how the app brings clarity to your thoughts and ideas.

*   **Lucent Notes**
    *   *Meaning:* Glowing with or giving off light; clear.
    *   *UI Theme Influence:* High-contrast dark mode with "glowing" neon accents. Drop shadows are replaced with subtle outer glows. Typography is incredibly crisp and legible.
*   **Prism Canvas**
    *   *Meaning:* A medium that clarifies and separates light.
    *   *UI Theme Influence:* Dark theme, but the drawing tools (pens, highlighters) are vibrant and act as the primary source of color. Subtle iridescent or holographic effects on premium UI elements (like the Pro paywall).

## Direction 3: Abstract & Premium (One-Word Brands)
One-word Latin or abstract names often feel the most premium and luxury (think *Notion*, *Obsidian*, *Figma*).

*   **Aperio**
    *   *Meaning:* Latin for "to uncover, reveal, or open."
    *   *UI Theme Influence:* Highly sophisticated, luxury UI. Matte finishes, muted gold or copper accents against deep charcoal backgrounds. Typography feels editorial (perhaps mixing a serif for headings with our current DM Sans for UI).
*   **Caelum**
    *   *Meaning:* Latin for "the heavens" or "sky."
    *   *UI Theme Influence:* Soft, airy, and expansive. Even in dark mode, the backgrounds are soft navy blues rather than harsh blacks.

## How to Handle Copyright and Trademarks

To ensure you don't run into copyright/trademark issues, follow this checklist before finalizing a name:

1.  **USPTO Search (US Patent and Trademark Office):** Go to [uspto.gov](https://www.uspto.gov/) and use their TESS search tool. Search for your proposed name in **Class 009** (Software/Mobile Apps) and **Class 042** (Software as a Service).
2.  **App Store / Google Play Search:** Literally search the exact name in both stores. If an app with the exact name exists (even if it's dead), it can cause confusion or rejection during the app review process.
3.  **Domain Name Check:** Check if the `.app`, `.io`, or `.com` is available. (e.g., `aethera.app` or `lucentnotes.com`).
4.  **GitHub / Product Hunt Search:** Check to see if open-source projects or startups have recently launched with the name.
