| **App Name** | **Horizon Notes** |

| **Short Pitch** | A premium, infinite-canvas note-taking app that gives you the space to think, with seamless cross-platform sync. |

---

## 2️⃣ Why "Horizon"?

1. **The Meaning:** A horizon represents the edge of the visible world—something that is always expanding and never truly reachable. It is the perfect metaphor for an infinite canvas where you never hit the edge of the page.
2. **The Market Positioning:** It sounds expansive and limitless, directly attacking the biggest weakness of Samsung Notes and GoodNotes (which trap users in restrictive, physical A4 page borders).
3. **The Sound:** It is a strong, 3-syllable word that sounds premium, expensive, and professional. It sits perfectly alongside top-tier productivity tools like Notion, Superhuman, and Obsidian.
4. **Availability:** Web and App Store searches confirm the name is completely clear in the consumer productivity and note-taking space.

---

## 3️⃣ Visual Language (Neo-Skeuomorphic — "Tactile Blueprint")

To match the "Horizon" name — limitless, expansive, and grounded — our UI follows a **Neo-Skeuomorphic** design language:

*   **Paper & Ink:** Warm off-white paper surfaces (`#F4F2EC`) with dark ink (`#2D2D2D`) borders and text. The UI feels like a hand-drawn wireframe come to life.
*   **Tactile Surfaces:** Interactive elements use raised/pressed/inset states with crisp 2px ink borders. Buttons physically "push down" into the paper when active (darker inset background, shadow removed).
*   **Monochromatic Palette:** No accent colors — `primary` is just ink. Hierarchy is achieved through weight, size, and depth, not color variation.
*   **Typography:** System-native font with three weight tokens (Regular 400, Medium 500, Bold 700). Clean, platform-appropriate, no custom web fonts.
*   **Icons:** Flutter Material Icons in ink color (active) or muted grey (inactive).
*   **No Glassmorphism:** No backdrop blur, no frosted glass, no transparency effects. Every surface is opaque paper.
