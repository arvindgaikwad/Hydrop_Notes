💡 The Golden Rule of Gating

The canvas itself is always fully infinite and free. We gate the tools (highlighter, full colors, brush sizes) — not the canvas. Free users fall in love with the space but want better tools. The canvas is the hook, the tools are the upsell.

3 Critical Performance Optimizations

1. Stroke Rasterization

After pen lifts, "bake" strokes into static bitmaps. Old strokes cost ZERO GPU cycles.

2. Viewport Culling

Only render strokes visible on screen. 100 or 10,000 strokes = same performance.

3. Level of Detail (LOD)

At low zoom, swap vectors for low-res bitmaps. Full detail only when zoomed in.

10 — Risk Assessment
Risks & Mitigations
Three key risks with clear mitigation strategies.
⚠️

Performance at Scale

Infinite canvas with heavy content could lag on older devices.

MITIGATION

Stroke rasterization + viewport culling + LOD from Day 1. Upgrade to native PencilKit/SurfaceView in V1.1 if needed.

⚠️

Sync Reliability

Sync failure is the graveyard of note apps. Data loss = dead app.

MITIGATION

Page-level sync (not notebook). Conflict = fork, never overwrite. Local-first architecture means offline always works.

⚠️

User Acquisition

Entering a crowded market with established players.

MITIGATION

Target Samsung Notes users losing sync in July '26. SEO + content marketing around "Samsung Notes alternative." Exploit subscription fatigue.
