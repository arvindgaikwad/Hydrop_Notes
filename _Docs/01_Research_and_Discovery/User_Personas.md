Based on our competitor analysis and customer review deep-dive, these are the two primary users we are designing for.

---

## 🎓 Persona 1: "The Spatially Constrained Student"

**Name:** Maya Patel  
**Age:** 21  
**Occupation:** Medical Student  
**Primary Devices:** iPad Pro 11", Windows Laptop (Dell XPS), iPhone 14  

### 🎯 Goals
- Needs to create massive, interconnected mind maps for biology and anatomy.
- Wants to import 100+ page PDF textbooks and annotate in the margins without running out of space.
- Needs all notes to sync instantly to her Windows laptop so she can study on a larger screen.

### 😫 Frustrations (Pain Points)
- **The GoodNotes Lag:** Her iPad overheats and the app crashes when her biology whiteboard gets too large.
- **The Page Boundary:** She hates having to draw an arrow to "continued on next page" when a flowchart gets too wide in Notability.
- **Sync Failure:** She tried OneNote, but her iPad notes randomly failed to sync before a major exam (Error 0xE0001475).

### 💡 What We Must Deliver for Maya
- A completely fluid, lag-free infinite canvas where she can pan infinitely in any direction.
- A rock-solid sync engine to her Windows laptop.
- An affordable price (students don't want a $100/yr subscription).

---

## 💼 Persona 2: "The Ecosystem-Stranded Professional"

**Name:** David Chen  
**Age:** 34  
**Occupation:** Product Manager  
**Primary Devices:** Samsung Galaxy Z Fold, Samsung Galaxy Tab S9, MacBook Pro (Work)  

### 🎯 Goals
- Wants a clean, minimalist UI that doesn't feel like a cluttered enterprise app.
- Needs to quickly sketch wireframes, product flows, and meeting notes.
- Requires deep, nested folders to organize notes by Quarter > Project > Feature.

### 😫 Frustrations (Pain Points)
- **The Impending Doom:** He uses Samsung Notes, but since Samsung is killing OneNote sync in July 2026, he will lose access to his notes on his work MacBook.
- **Messy Alternatives:** He tried OneNote, but hates how messy the tabs and sections become as a project scales. 
- **Missing Features:** He tried Nebo, but their folder system is too shallow (only 1 level deep) for his project management needs.

### 💡 What We Must Deliver for David
- A universal, web/Mac/Windows accessible sync that frees him from the Samsung ecosystem.
- A "Samsung-level" deep folder organization system.
- A one-time purchase option. He hates renting software for his personal tools.
